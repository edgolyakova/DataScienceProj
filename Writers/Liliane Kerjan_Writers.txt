Liliane Kerjan (13 February 1940 – 28 June 2021) was a French author. She specialized in American literature.

Biography
Kerjan earned a degree in English from Rennes 2 University and a doctoral degree from Blaise Pascal University in 1977. She would go on to serve as Vice-President of Rennes 2 and subsequently was Rector of the Académie de Limoges from 2000 to 2005. As part of the Fulbright Program, she was a visiting professor at the University of San Diego and Yale University.
In 1986, Kerjan began her collaboration with La Quinzaine littéraire. In 2010, she published a biography on Tennessee Williams and another on Arthur Miller in 2012.  She was also President of the Institut franco-américain de Rennes from 2007 to 2020. In 2020, she published Ils ont fait un rêve, a biography on African American authors Richard Wright, Ralph Ellison, and James Baldwin.Liliane Kerjan died on 28 June 2021 at the age of 81.

Awards and honours
In 2010, she received the Prix grand Ouest from the Association des Écrivains de l'Ouest.
