Richard Jorif (1930–2010) was a French writer. He was born to an Indian father and a mother from Martinique. He published his first novel at the age of 57. The book titled Le Navire Argo won the Alain Fournier Prize. Other books include “Clownerie” (François Bourin, 1988), “Paul Valéry” (Lattès, 1991), and “Tohu-bohu” (Julliard, 2000). His last book in 2001 was a collection of short stories.
