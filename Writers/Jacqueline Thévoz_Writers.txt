Jacqueline Thévoz (29 April 1926 – 29 January 2021) was a Swiss writer, poet, and journalist. Biography Thévoz attended the University of Lausanne, where she studied political science, and won the Follope Prize in 1949. She simultaneously studied music and choreography at the Conservatoire Lausanne-Paris. She began her career as a journalist. She also had a career as a choreographer and music composer. She published multiple novels, plays, and collections of poems and essays, and was active in several writing associations, including the Association vaudoise des écrivains and the Société des gens de lettres. In her later years she lived in the French commune of Larringes near Évian, close to the Swiss border.Jacqueline Thévoz died on 29 January 2021 at the age of 94.
