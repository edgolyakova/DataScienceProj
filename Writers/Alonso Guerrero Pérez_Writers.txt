Alonso Guerrero Pérez (Almendralejo, Badajoz) on 12 November 1962) is a Spanish writer and professor. In addition to fiction, he has also written essays, literary criticism, and journalism. Life Born in Almendralejo (Badajoz), after his university studies he became a high school teacher. He was awarded some literature prizes: the prestigious "Premio Felipe Trigo" from Villanueva de la Serena (Badajoz) for his Tricotomía, in 1982; and the Premio Navarra de Novela for his Los años imaginarios in 1987. On 7 August 1998 Alonso Guerrero married Letizia Ortiz, a Spanish journalist, in a simple civil ceremony at Almendralejo, after a 10-year courtship; they divorced in 1999, however. Ortiz would go on to marry the future King Felipe VI, thus becoming Queen of Spain.
